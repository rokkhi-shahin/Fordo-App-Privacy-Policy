Last update: September 13, 2022
1. Introduction:
Thank you for using Fordo Services! We are committed to providing you with the best
services. This Privacy Policy explains what information “Fordo” collects, how that
information is used, and the choices you can make about that information.
2. Information We Collect:
A. Information You Provide to Us:
At various points in the Fordo experience, you may provide us with information about
yourself. For example, when you create an account through the Services, you provide us with
personal information like your name, email address, and phone number. You may also
provide information about your gender, age, and password required to log into the app after
signup.
The App requests permission to access the phone’s storage only to store images and the
recorded audio ﬁle and play the recorded ﬁles afterward. The App does not access any other
ﬁles or excess user information like text ﬁles, videos, or documents except the app-produced
or stored media ﬁles only.
We collect device information (phone model, screen ratio, network status, network location,
date & time) from the device. This information is collected to optimize the “Fordo”
application and increase its performance via the latest updates and patches. This
information cannot be shared with other users. Sharing a fordo list will not send the device
information to another user.
Recording Audio - Microphone:
Fordo provides users with voice control features. By activating voice controls, you enable
features such as searching for items using your voice. Voice control is only available for
logged-in users. When you activate voice features, your voice input and other information will
be sent to Fordo Server. This includes audio recordings and transcripts of what you say.
Fordo also allows users to upload recordings of their voice as audio notes, or to help Fordo
improve its voice control features. These recordings and their transcripts will similarly be
stored by Fordo.
Fordo uses this data to recognize what you say, understand you better, provide you with the
requested service, and/or take an action. Fordo may also use this data in other ways, for
example, using your data to improve voice functionality on Fordo and to develop new voice
features.
Fordo will begin receiving your voice input data when you press the action button or say the
wake word (for voice search) when you hear an audible tone, and continue until Fordo has
processed your search.
The device will indicate to you when Fordo is receiving your voice input, for example with a
visual indicator or an audible tone.
When listening for the wake word, Fordo listens in short snippets of a few seconds which are
deleted if the wake word is not detected.
Users can delete audio recordings that we store on our servers. For audio recordings added
to a list as an audio note, the app provides features to delete the note or the whole list. For
recordings of voice features, we provide a feature in the app to delete these recordings.
For recordings made of users' use of the voice controls (such as audio search), you may
request to delete those recordings by emailing us here at fordosupport@scitechbd.com.
Location Information:
When you use the services, we may collect location data. For instance, if you allow the
services to access location services through the permission system used by your device's
mobile operating system, we may collect the precise location of your device. We use your
location information to assist you in ﬁnding nearby stores or places to start an
alert/reminder, for other similar purposes, and for analytics purposes. You can choose
whether or not to enable the location tracking feature through the settings on your device, or
when prompted by the “Fordo” mobile app. We may also infer your general location
information, for example by using your internet protocol (IP) address. You can turn off
location data collection by denying location permission from the dialog box that appears on
the Fordo app home screen or you also can turn off location from the phone settings of the
device you are using.

identiﬁers, screens that you navigate to and app features that you click, searches that you
run on the Services, and other ways you interact with the Services. If you are logged into the
Services, this information is stored with your account information.
